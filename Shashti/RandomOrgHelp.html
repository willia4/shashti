Ordinary computers are unable to generate actual random numbers and must therefore make do with what are called "pseudo-random numbers".

This application uses the pseudo-random number generator provided by the system for cryptographic services. This generator is seeded with actual external entropy and is more than suitable for password-generating purposes.

However, if you are concerned, this application can use random numbers from RANDOM.ORG.

RANDOM.ORG is the primary source of true randomness on the Internet and is thus under heavy load. They have therefore implemented a quota system. This application attempts to be a good citizen and should not stress your RANDOM.ORG quotas.

If, however, RANDOM.ORG stops responding, you should consider reverting to the built-in pseudo-random generator.
